GM... Oh!

  • Here are a few things to consider
    before you reach for that GMO-free tomato.

    GMO (Genetically Modified Organism), also known as a transgenic organism,
    is any organism whose genetic material has been altered
    using genetic engineering techniques. - Wikipedia

    Use your up and down keys to navigate!

1. It's complicated.

GMOs are not evil. A GMO label may not tell you that
the apple you're holding was sprayed with dangerous herbicides.
At the same time, you can have an organic, genetically
engineered apple that's delicious and safe to eat.

2. Genetically modified food is nothing new.

In fact, we have been selectively breeding species for more than 15,000 years.
As astrophysicist and science communicator Neil Degrasse Tyson reminds us,
"Practically every food you buy in the store for consumption
by humans is genetically modified food." See how it's quite pointless to go anti-GMO?

3. There is NO substantial evidence that
GMOs are unsafe to consume.

The World Health Organization, the American Medical Association, the National
Academy of Sciences, and the American Association for the Advancement
of Science, among others, have assured the safety of GMOs.

Take a look at this graphic that shows which scientific organisations around
the world have mentioned the safety of crop biotechnology.

4. GMO-free does not mean safe, healthy, and all around #FABULOUS

"But 'GMO-free' does not mean fair trade, and it does not mean sustainable,
and it does not mean monoculture-averting, and it does not mean
rainforest-enabling, and it does not mean labor-friendly, and it does not
mean healthy, though it puffs its chest and carries itself alongside those claims."

- James Hamblin, The Atlantic

5. GMO labels don’t clarify what’s in your food.

"They don’t address the underlying ingredients—pesticides, toxins, proteins—that supposedly make GMOs harmful. They stigmatize food that’s perfectly safe, and they deflect scrutiny from non-GMO products that have the same disparaged ingredients." - William Saletan, Slate

6. Don't be intimidated by the science.

Just because GMOs are now made in the lab, does not mean that all GMOs
are inherently unhealthy or dangerous. If you find yourself saying, "The science
behind GMOs are too complicated, and I can't bother to deal with all of this!",
embrace the uncertainty and learn more about the issue.

More knowledge never hurts, especially when it
concerns your health and the health of the environment!

7. Don't blame the GMO. Blame our egos.

Anti-GMO activists often attribute superpests and herbicide-resistant weeds
to GMOs, however, the problem actually lies in relying on the same crop and same method of weed control over and over again.

"The worst you could say, is that having these superstar, genetically modified plants
may create a perverse incentive for monoculture because then everybody
wants to use the most profitable, best seeds."

- Dr. Steven Novella, Assistant Professor of Neurology, Yale University
Inquiring Minds Podcast

8. Demand better GMOs.

Getting rid of GMOs won't get rid of food covered with dangerous pesticides.
Creating better GMOs and letting go of our fear of genetic engineering may lead to healthier foods.
Educate yourself on the latest findings, talk to farmers at your local
farmers market, communicate to your policymakers that you want better GMOs,
and educate the people around you.
